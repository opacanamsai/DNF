Tips for Registering Cold Chain Transportation

How to Prepare Goods for Cold Chain Transport?

Transporting frozen goods requires meticulous preparation to ensure quality and safety. Below are some helpful tips to prepare your goods before transportation:

  • 01

    Choose the Right Packaging

    • Use High-Quality Packaging: Opt for specialized packaging for frozen goods, such as styrofoam boxes, vacuum bags, or insulated cartons. This helps protect the goods from external temperature fluctuations.
    • Ensure Proper Fit: Use packaging that fits the goods snugly to minimize empty space, which helps maintain stable temperatures.
  • 02

    Ensure Goods Are Properly Frozen

    • Pre-Freeze Goods: Make sure the goods are frozen at the appropriate temperature before transportation. For food items, the ideal temperature is typically -18°C or lower.
    • Check the Temperature: Use a thermometer to verify the temperature of the goods before packing to ensure they meet the required freezing conditions.
  • 03

    Use Clear Labels and Special Markings

    • Clear Labeling: Attach labels or notes to each package with details about the type of goods, storage temperature, and production dates.
    • Special Markings: For critical shipments, use special tags or symbols (e.g., "Frozen Goods," "Do Not Thaw") to ensure that logistics staff handle them with care.

Things to Note When Booking Cold Chain Transport Services

  • Provide Accurate Information: Ensure you provide precise details about the volume, type of goods, and destination. This allows the transport provider to plan and prepare more effectively while ensuring your goods are handled under the right conditions.
  • Schedule Transport During Low-Risk Periods: Avoid scheduling transport on weekends or holidays, as high traffic during these times can cause delays and affect product quality.
  • Consider Cargo Insurance Services: Research cargo insurance options to safeguard your goods against risks like damage or loss during transportation.

Effective Cold Storage Practices

5 Steps to Safely Store Frozen Goods Before Transport

  • 01

    Check the Temperature of Freezers or Cold Storage

    • Ensure the freezer or cold storage is set to the optimal temperature, typically -18°C or lower, before storing goods. Maintaining a stable temperature is crucial for preserving frozen goods.
  • 02

    Ensure Proper Air Circulation in Storage

    • Good airflow helps maintain even temperatures within the storage area. Avoid blocking air vents and keep goods away from walls to promote efficient air circulation.
  • 03

    Label Each Product’s Ideal Storage Temperature

    • Clearly label each type of product with its required storage temperature to help staff manage and store goods appropriately.
  • 04

    Organize Goods Logically

    • Arrange products using the "First In, First Out" (FIFO) method. Place items nearing their expiration date in accessible positions to reduce waste and optimize storage.
  • 05

    Regularly Inspect Expiry Dates

    • Periodically check product expiration dates and remove any expired items to maintain overall quality and safety.

Common Mistakes in Preserving Frozen Goods

  • Overcrowding Storage Space: Packing too many items in a confined area can disrupt airflow, leading to uneven freezing and potential spoilage.
  • Neglecting Regular Temperature Checks: Factors like power outages or equipment failure can cause temperature fluctuations. Frequent checks ensure products are stored correctly.
  • Using Non-Airtight Packaging: Poor-quality packaging may allow warm air to enter, compromising product quality. Always use specialized, airtight materials for frozen goods.
