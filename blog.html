Requirements for Transporting Frozen Goods

Transporting frozen goods requires special requirements to ensure quality and safety. Here are some important factors:

  • 01

    Transport Temperature

    Frozen goods must be maintained at low temperatures, usually 0 degrees Celsius or below, throughout the transport process to avoid damage and loss of quality.

  • 02

    Specialized Vehicles

    Specialized vehicles with temperature control systems must be used to preserve frozen goods throughout the journey, ensuring that food quality is not changed. Only when these requirements are followed can frozen goods arrive safely and maintain optimal quality.

Important Notes for Transporting Frozen Goods

Transporting frozen goods requires care and professionalism to ensure that product quality is not affected. The following important factors will help you transport safely and effectively:

  • 01

    Properly Pack Goods

    Packing frozen goods correctly is the first step in protecting products from external factors. Goods should be packed in foam boxes or specialized coolers. To maintain a stable temperature, these boxes need to be supplemented with ice or cooling gel. In addition, using vacuum bags or moisture-proof protective layers will help keep the goods from being damaged by moisture. Once packaged, you need to check the goods again, making sure there are no gaps that cause temperature changes.

  • 02

    Continuous Temperature Control

    Temperature is an important factor that determines the quality of frozen goods. Each type of product has different temperature requirements, which can range from -22°C to -12°C depending on the characteristics of the product. Monitoring and maintaining temperature during transportation is mandatory. Before transportation, the refrigeration equipment must be carefully checked, and during the transportation, the temperature must be monitored regularly. Specialized temperature measuring devices must be used to ensure stability and no abnormal temperature changes.

  • 03

    Scientific Arrangement of Goods

    It is also important to arrange goods properly on the means of transport to ensure preservation efficiency. Products need to have enough space for cold air to circulate evenly. Avoid arranging them too densely, causing cold air to not be able to reach all products. At the same time, it is necessary to ensure that the coolers are not skewed or have gaps between them, as this can reduce preservation efficiency. A well-ventilated transportation space will help maintain a more stable temperature during transportation.

  • 04

    Use Specialized Transport Vehicles

    To ensure the smooth transportation of frozen goods, the means of transport must be equipped with a refrigeration system that can adjust the temperature. Especially for long-distance shipments or shipments passing through many areas with changing ambient temperatures, the refrigeration system must operate continuously and effectively. In addition, trucks, containers, or refrigerated containers also need to be cleaned and maintained regularly to ensure that no unexpected incidents occur during transportation.

  • 05

    Shorten Transportation Time

    The shorter the transportation time, the better the product is preserved. Shipping units often choose the shortest route, the most efficient means of transport, and avoid areas with high traffic congestion to minimize transportation time. Especially for long-distance orders, logistics companies need to have a specific plan, including choosing the departure and destination points so that the delivery process is quick and smooth.
